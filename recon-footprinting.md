# Footprinting

Footprinting is the first step in penetration testing where you collect information about your target (such as an organization or network). It means gathering details about the target’s systems, networks, and security setup.

Footprinting is the process of gathering information about a target organization before launching an attack. 

Below are the key techniques attackers use:
* Footprinting is the first step in ethical hacking or penetration testing. It means gathering information about a target system or organization before launching an attack. The goal is to understand the external environment and identify potential weaknesses in Azure cloud services, networks, or resources.
* Footprinting: First step in evaluation of security posture of target organization. It will provide security profile blueprint of organization.
* Footprinting: The First Step
* Footprinting is the initial stage of reconnaissance, focusing on collecting information about the target organization's external security posture. It helps attackers create a "security profile blueprint" of the target.

### Footprinting Methods:
* Passive Footprinting: Gathering information without directly interacting with the target. This involves searching public sources like social media, job boards, and search engines.
* Active Footprinting: Directly interacting with the target to gather information. This can involve techniques like traceroute analysis, querying nameservers, or scanning public web portals.

**Benefits of Footprinting for Attackers:**
* Understanding Security Posture: Assess the target's overall security strength and weaknesses.
* Reduced Attack Scope: Narrow down the attack focus to specific areas like IP addresses, networks, or services.
* Identifying Vulnerabilities: Discover exploitable weaknesses in systems to choose the most effective attack methods.
* Mapping the Network: Create a layout of the target network infrastructure to understand its architecture.


Beyond Footprinting: Other Reconnaissance Techniques
While footprinting is the first step, reconnaissance also includes other techniques:
* Network Scanning: Actively probing a network to identify devices and vulnerabilities.
* •	Enumeration: Gathering detailed information about specific systems and services on the network.
* •	Vulnerability Assessment: Identifying and evaluating potential weaknesses in systems and applications.
* By combining these techniques, attackers can build a comprehensive picture of the target organization's security posture, making them more likely to launch a successful attack.

Footprinting is the initial phase of an attack where attackers gather information about a target organization. This information helps them:
* •	Understand the security posture: Identify weaknesses in the target's external defenses.
* •	Focus their efforts: Narrow down potential attack points like IP addresses, networks, and vulnerabilities.
* •	Craft targeted attacks: Choose exploits that are most likely to succeed against the target's systems.
* •	Map the network: Gain a high-level view of the target's network infrastructure.

Types of Footprinting:
* •	Passive Footprinting: Gathering information without directly interacting with the target. Techniques include:
* o	Searching public sources like search engines, social media, job boards, and the deep web.
* o	Analyzing the target's online reputation.
* •	Active Footprinting: Directly interacting with the target to gather information. Techniques include:
* o	Tracing network routes (traceroute).
* o	Querying public nameservers.
* o	Extracting data from publicly available documents and files.
* o	Scanning web portals.
* o	Social engineering tactics.
* o	Extracting DNS information.
